Methodology

  • Coverage = (In Deployment + Technical Go-Live + Go-Live − Live with savings) / Total In Scope
  • Conversion = (Technical Go-Live + Go-Live − Live with savings) / Total In Scope
  • Overall regional score = 40% Coverage + 60% Conversion
  • Initiative contribution is weighted by implementation priority within Fab.Operate, so the ranking emphasizes the initiatives that matter most for the transformation agenda.
Interpretation for CEOs: the ranking should be read as a transformation execution lens, not just a deployment count. Regions score higher when they move a larger share of in-scope accounts into deployable and convertible states, especially in the initiatives with the highest implementation priority.

Executive insights for CEO review

The portfolio is shifting from deployment readiness to value realization.

Compared with the June 8 cut, the June 22 comments show that several regions now have enough live or technical go-live activity to move the discussion beyond implementation status. The main CEO question should now be whether those deployments are producing measurable operational and financial return.

Global dependencies remain the main source of execution drag.

Across WFM, Supervisors, Training, and QA, the same blockers repeat: BMS connection, India-owned updates, tenant/licensing issues, legal/subprocessor approvals, and client-side approvals. These are not local operational gaps only; they are structural dependencies that require coordinated escalation.

Coverage is no longer enough; conversion quality matters more.

The ranking already weights coverage and conversion differently, and the updated comments reinforce why. Some regions have broad activity, but much of it remains in pre-work, technical go-live, or approval stages. CEO attention should favor regions and initiatives that are converting into sustained business outcomes.

The strongest next management lens is ROI by region and by initiative.

The comments now make clear that implementation alone is not the end objective. US-Canada is explicitly flagging ROI concerns; NCA and CSC still need to prove WFM value beyond technical progress; MAR and BRA have stronger proof points that should be validated and potentially scaled.

NCA

Overall rank #1 · Strongest initiatives: Product Training (14.0), Centrical (12.7), Hallo (11.4)

NCA ranks #1, supported by Product Training, Centrical, and RTM Bot, but value realization is still uneven.

The updated leader inputs confirm real execution traction in Supervisors and WFM, especially in Mexico, El Salvador, and Guatemala. However, the region is still carrying a major systems dependency: Datanativ remains constrained by the BMS connection, and QA is still largely in pre-work or value-case validation. In other words, NCA is leading the ranking, but not all of that lead has converted into realized operational value yet.

Management implication: Prioritize two decisions: unblock the BMS dependency for WFM and sharpen the commercial value case for EMA QA where clients already have analytics tools.

Action item: Action item: review the ROI of the transformation in NCA by initiative, especially Centrical, RTM Bot, and Datanativ, to confirm where technical progress is already translating into savings.
