Fab.Operate Americas CEO Ranking

Reporting cut: June 9, 2026 · Ranking source: Jun09 - AMS - Fab.Operate Ranking.xlsx · Leadership comment source: May25 - AMS - Fab.Operate Leaders comments.xlsx

This CEO ranking provides an executive view of Fab.Operate execution across the Americas. The current cut shows a clear split between regions building breadth and regions converting that breadth into implementation outcomes. As of June 9, BRA ranks first, followed by MAR and CSC, while South Cone and US-Canada continue to show larger conversion gaps despite important pipeline activity.

Methodology. Overall ranking combines 40% Coverage and 60% Conversion. Coverage is calculated as accounts in deployment, technical go-live, and go-live divided by total in-scope accounts. Conversion is calculated as technical go-live and go-live divided by total in-scope accounts. Initiative contributions are then weighted by implementation priority to build the regional score.

Portfolio signals requiring CEO attention

  • Product Training remains the largest weighted execution gap: it carries the highest priority weight in the model, but still shows low conversion and high not-started exposure in every sub-region.
  • EMA QA is still a constrained quality workstream: coverage remains low, conversion is near zero in most regions, and leader comments continue to point to legal/client-approval dependencies.
  • Workforce is the clearest source of differentiated regional performance: CSC, US-Canada, NCA, and MAR all show meaningful contribution from Datanativ or RTM Bot, while BRA and South Cone still require sharper value validation.
  • Hallo remains the cleanest scale proof point in the portfolio, already live at high maturity in BRA, MAR, CSC, and NCA.

Leadership comments linked to results

This section summarizes leader comments by region and connects them to the current ranking results so the report can support strategic decisions, not only status reporting.

BRA

Rank #1 · Coverage 28.4 · Conversion 20.0 · Overall 23.4
Comment coverage: Quality, Recruitment, Training, Workforce

Result signal

Top weighted contributors: Product Training (Training), RTM Bot (Workforce). Main pressure points in current results: Product Training (Training), Centrical (Supervisors).

Leadership view

Overall leadership is being sustained by strong Recruiting performance, an early but measurable Product Training pipeline, and Workforce rollout now under active implementation.

Main blocker

Leader comments point to three blockers: Datanativ still lacks key integrations for Brazil operations, global training programs limit reduction opportunities, and QA progress depends on client response. No Supervisors update was reported for the week of May 25.

Decision implication

BRA is performing well overall, but part of that strength sits on initiatives already scaled. Leadership attention should focus on unlocking the next wave of value in Workforce, QA, and the missing Supervisors workstream.

Recommended next move: Prioritize a decision on Datanativ licensing/integration, protect QA scope discipline, and request an updated BRA Supervisors plan before the next CEO cut.
Missing update: Supervisors update missing for BRA (week of May 25).

MAR

Rank #2 · Coverage 29.8 · Conversion 17.3 · Overall 22.3
Comment coverage: Quality, Recruitment, Training, Workforce

Result signal

Top weighted contributors: Product Training (Training), Hallo (Recruitment). Main pressure points in current results: Product Training (Training), Centrical (Supervisors).

Leadership view

MAR remains one of the most balanced regions, with strong Hiring performance, active Workforce progress, and a large Training pipeline already commercialized.

Main blocker

The main blocker is not top-of-funnel activity but conversion: Training still has an approval bottleneck, QA is slowed by legal/addendum cycles, and several accounts depend on faster global governance decisions.

Decision implication

MAR has enough pipeline to sustain leadership, but without tighter approval management the region risks carrying breadth without converting it into enough live deployments.

Recommended next move: Use weekly executive tracking to close approvals, escalate global dependencies quickly, and convert the strongest Training and QA opportunities into technical go-live decisions.

CSC

Rank #3 · Coverage 27.2 · Conversion 16.7 · Overall 20.9
Comment coverage: Quality, Recruitment, Supervisors, Training, Workforce

Result signal

Top weighted contributors: RTM Bot (Workforce), Hallo (Recruitment). Main pressure points in current results: Centrical (Supervisors), Product Training (Training).

Leadership view

CSC moved into the top three mainly on the back of Workforce, where Datanativ is the region’s clearest scaled execution signal; Training also continues to add new programs.

Main blocker

The comments show uneven maturity across workstreams: Training remains pitch-heavy, Workforce still requires significant manual effort, and QA/Supervisors progress is still earlier-stage than the ranking leaders.

Decision implication

CSC has a credible execution story, but the next strategic question is whether the region can broaden success beyond Workforce and avoid concentration risk in a single workstream.

Recommended next move: Keep exploiting Workforce as the anchor proof point, while forcing a sharper conversion plan in Training and tightening the deployment path for QA and Supervisors.

NCA

Rank #4 · Coverage 22.0 · Conversion 16.7 · Overall 18.8
Comment coverage: Quality, Recruitment, Supervisors, Training, Workforce

Result signal

Top weighted contributors: Centrical (Supervisors), Hallo (Recruitment). Main pressure points in current results: Ema QA (Quality), Product Training (Training).

Leadership view

NCA shows the strongest operating momentum in Supervisors through Centrical, alongside full Hiring rollout and relevant progress in Workforce.

Main blocker

Comments indicate that global deployment strategy is still unclear for several multinational clients, AlphaRun contracts continue to delay Recruiting value, and Training remains highly dependent on client approval and credential readiness.

Decision implication

NCA is execution-capable, but more of its pipeline now needs structural unblockers from Global rather than additional commercial activity.

Recommended next move: Clarify the global playbook for multinational clients, protect Recruiting economics until AlphaRun sunsets, and force targeted approval closures in Training to improve conversion.

South Cone

Rank #5 · Coverage 34.0 · Conversion 5.8 · Overall 17.1
Comment coverage: Quality, Recruitment, Supervisors, Training, Workforce

Result signal

Top weighted contributors: Centrical (Supervisors), Hallo (Recruitment). Main pressure points in current results: Centrical (Supervisors), RTM Bot (Workforce).

Leadership view

South Cone has the highest coverage in the ranking and strong early movement in Supervisors, plus initial Workforce and Recruiting traction.

Main blocker

That breadth is not yet translating into conversion. Centrical coverage is high but conversion is still zero, local teams question the cost logic of global WFM tools, and Training progress is concentrated in Argentina. No Training update was reported for Chile in the week of May 25.

Decision implication

South Cone is the clearest example of breadth without enough conversion. The key decision is whether blockers are executional and solvable now, or whether the current pipeline needs scope reprioritization.

Recommended next move: Move Supervisors accounts from deployment to technical go-live, validate the South Cone business case for WFM tools, and request a Chile-specific Training update to complete the regional view.
Missing update: Training update missing for Chile in South Cone (week of May 25).

US-Canada

Rank #6 · Coverage 20.5 · Conversion 9.5 · Overall 13.9
Comment coverage: Quality, Supervisors, Workforce

Result signal

Top weighted contributors: RTM Bot (Workforce), Hallo (Recruitment). Main pressure points in current results: Product Training (Training).

Leadership view

US-Canada continues to show strong signals in Workforce and some Hiring progress, but the region remains constrained in the overall ranking by low conversion in higher-priority tools.

Main blocker

The leadership comments point to a structural blocker: legal validation is still gating Supervisors deployment at scale, while QA also depends on client approvals and support from EMA for deployment speed.

Decision implication

US-Canada does not need more pipeline generation first; it needs blocker removal. Until legal and client-approval issues are resolved, conversion will remain disproportionately low versus the region’s addressable base.

Recommended next move: Resolve legal classification rules quickly, accelerate license provisioning and data integration, and keep a focused list of QA accounts that can move fastest once approvals clear.
