MAR
Workstreams covered: Training, QA, WFM, Recruiting, Supervisors
Summary
MAR continues to show the broadest and most advanced activity across Training and QA, with Recruiting already live and Supervisors progressing through activation and client pitches. The region has a sizeable pipeline and several accounts already positioned to move with focused intervention.
Main challenge
The main constraint remains approvals and legal/compliance gates, which are slowing conversion from identified opportunities into broader deployment. Some accounts also require stronger executive sponsorship to move from pilot interest to adoption.
Next step
Prioritize the pending approvals, keep weekly escalation on stalled decisions, and continue fast-track pitches and pilot standardization to convert the active pipeline.
BRA
Workstreams covered: Training, QA, WFM, Recruiting
Summary
Brazil shows a healthy pipeline across Training and WFM, while Hallo AI is already live and QA continues to advance through targeted client opportunities. The region has good underlying momentum but remains earlier-stage in conversion than MAR.
Main challenge
The main blocker is legal and client addendum timing for QA, while WFM still depends on product readiness and rollout maturity to move from approached status into later stages.
Next step
Convert the strongest open opportunities, close the pending legal/addendum items, and continue presenting Fab.Operate solutions to new accounts to widen deployable pipeline.
NCA
Workstreams covered: Supervisors, QA, WFM, Recruiting, Training
Summary
NCA combines active Centrical pilots in Mexico with broader early-stage activity across CAN in QA, Training, WFM, Recruiting, and Supervisors. The region has meaningful pipeline volume, but much of it is still concentrated in pre-work or approached stages.
Main challenge
The main regional blocker is the lack of a clear global deployment and legal path for global clients, which continues to delay Centrical, EMA, and some training and recruiting conversions. Contract timing and financial dependencies also affect Hallo AI deployment.
Next step
Clarify the global deployment strategy, complete the pending legal reviews, and enforce commitment dates to move priority accounts out of pre-work and into deployment.
CSC
Workstreams covered: Training, QA, WFM, Recruiting, Supervisors
Summary
CSC has reactivated the training pipeline with new programs and already had active work across QA, Supervisors, Recruiting, and WFM in the prior cycle. The region is progressing, but most initiatives still require further enablement before scaling.
Main challenge
Resource capacity, training readiness, and dependency on alignment for global accounts remain the main execution constraints. In parallel, data integration and local tool enablement continue to be important for Supervisors and QA.
Next step
Complete the first client assessments and pitches, continue local enablement and data integration, and push the next set of commitments into active deployment.
South Cone
Workstreams covered: QA, Training, WFM, Recruiting, Supervisors
Summary
South Cone continues to advance across QA and Training, with EMA-related pilots, biometric use cases, and Fab Assist/Centrical activity already in motion. The region also has a broader base of opportunities across WFM, Recruiting, and Supervisors from the prior cycle.
Main challenge
Scaling is constrained by knowledge-base platform dependencies, local financial or business-case validation, and some legal or contract readiness items. In some cases, execution is moving, but the regional business case still needs confirmation before broader deployment.
Next step
Move current pilots into controlled validation, confirm the local business case for priority accounts, and continue expanding pitches where technical readiness is already in place.
US-Canada
Workstreams covered: Supervisors, QA, WFM, Recruiting, Training
Summary
US-Canada continues to progress on Centrical provisioning and data integration, while the prior cycle also showed active coverage across QA, WFM, Recruiting, and Training. The region has relevant volume in scope, but advancement remains highly dependent on central decisions.
Main challenge
The main blocker is the absence of clear legal definitions and deployment guidance for global accounts, which limits the ability to move a large set of accounts forward. Additional SME enablement is also still needed to support broader rollout.
Next step
Complete the legal validation path, secure the required SME train-the-trainer support, and continue onboarding the next wave of accounts once prerequisites are unlocked.
