Methodology

The overall ranking combines Coverage and Conversion, and then aggregates initiative results using priority weights assigned to each tool. This keeps the ranking simple while still reflecting where execution matters most.

Coverage

(In Deployment + Technical Go-Live + Go-Live) / Total In Scope

Measures how much of the addressable portfolio has already entered the implementation funnel.

Conversion

(Technical Go-Live + Go-Live) / Total In Scope

Measures how much of the addressable portfolio has advanced beyond preparation into execution-ready or live status.

Overall score

40% Coverage + 60% Conversion

Initiative scores are then combined using the priority weight assigned to each initiative in the ranking model.

  • SouthCone has the highest coverage in the portfolio, yet remains bottom-two because legal and contract closure prevent conversion.

Cross-workstream strategic insights from June 8 and June 22 leadership updates

Supervisors / Centrical

Execution is no longer blocked by lack of interest alone; the recurring blockers are tenant migration, user provisioning, KPI configuration and data readiness. NCA and SouthCone have stronger operational pipelines, while US-Canada is delayed by onboarding and warehouse dependencies. BRA lacks current reporting visibility.

Training

Onboarding and Pre-process are effectively normalized, so the remaining upside sits in Product Training. Across MAR, NCA, SouthCone and US-Canada, the dominant bottleneck is still client/legal approval rather than solution design.

Quality / Ema QA

Quality remains the weakest transformation lever outside MAR. The main pattern is consistent across June 8 and June 22: legal closure, migration from incumbent tools, metadata/SFTP issues and difficulty proving differentiated value when clients already use analytics platforms.

Workforce

RTM Bot is progressing, but Datanativ remains the clearest structural blocker in the portfolio. BMS dependency and manual work are repeated in comments across NCA, CSC, SouthCone and BRA. MAR is the closest region to stabilizing the tool and helping others move faster.

Recruitment / Hallo

Hallo is the most mature initiative in the portfolio. Most regions are already live or in sustainment, so the question has shifted from rollout to value realization and integration follow-through.

Regional strategic insights

These insights connect ranking results with what leaders reported in the June 8 and June 22 CEO status updates, so the section can be used to inform action and escalation rather than only describe status.

BRA Rank #1

BRA leads the ranking because execution is concentrated in a few high-performing initiatives, but the portfolio is still uneven.

BRA is #1 overall, driven by Product Training, RTM Bot and Hallo, all of which show meaningful implementation progress. Leadership comments also show operational improvement in WFM execution and readiness to support BMS-related work. The main gap is that this momentum is not yet broad-based: Datanativ still depends on BMS/manual work, QA is still waiting on client addenda, and there is no current supervisors update to explain why Centrical remains weak relative to the top regions.

Leadership implication: Protect BRA as a proof point on Training and Workforce, but force a targeted recovery plan for Supervisors and QA so that the region’s leadership is not dependent on only three initiatives.

Data note: No leadership update was available for BRA Supervisors in the June 8 / June 22 CEO status inputs.

NCA Rank #2

NCA is the strongest converter after BRA, but its next unlock depends on resolving structural execution blockers rather than opening more pipeline.

NCA ranks #2 overall, with strong scores in Centrical, Product Training, RTM Bot and Hallo. Comments from both June 8 and June 22 show a consistent pattern: Supervisors has real pipeline maturity, but tenant migration, KPI configuration and data integration still delay go-live; WFM remains constrained by Datanativ’s BMS dependency and manual work; QA is active in pre-work but still needs stronger differentiation versus client-owned analytics tools.

Leadership implication: Prioritize blocker removal over pipeline expansion: close BMS/Datanativ decisions, finish Centrical migration and KPI readiness, and sharpen the EMA QA business case for clients already using analytics.

MAR Rank #3

MAR remains one of the most balanced regions, with the strongest Quality position and meaningful execution depth, but approvals and platform dependencies still cap value capture.

MAR ranks #3 overall and is the current leader in Ema QA, while also holding strong positions in Product Training and Hallo. June 22 comments show MAR is closest to unlocking additional WFM value and is already supporting other regions, but RTM still depends on India-side updates and Datanativ requires version stability and admin fixes. Training comments also show a large approval backlog that still slows conversion from pitch to implementation.

Leadership implication: Use MAR as the regional benchmark for QA and as a support engine for Workforce, but escalate training approvals and India-dependent RTM items so execution can translate faster into scale.

CSC Rank #4

CSC has credible execution in Workforce and Hallo, but the portfolio still under-converts in the initiatives that matter most for broader transformation.

CSC ranks #4 overall. It stands out positively in Datanativ and RTM Bot, and Hallo is already live; however, Product Training remains weak and Ema QA has not yet converted into meaningful progress. The June 22 and June 8 leadership comments reinforce that QA is still mostly in approached/pre-work mode and Training is still working through legal/client approval and enablement readiness.

Leadership implication: Shift CSC attention from pipeline creation to conversion discipline in QA and Training; the region needs fewer early-stage discussions and more closure on client/legal approvals.

SouthCone Rank #5

SouthCone has the broadest coverage in the portfolio, but weak conversion keeps the region in the bottom tier.

SouthCone ranks #5 overall despite leading the Americas in coverage. The data and comments point to the same issue: this is not a pipeline problem, it is a closure problem. Centrical is the region’s strongest asset, but QA and Training are slowed by contract closure, legal approvals, subprocessor authorization, and migration from incumbent analytics solutions. WFM remains early and still tied to BMS/manual work assumptions.

Leadership implication: Treat SouthCone as a conversion-recovery case: accelerate legal/contract closure for QA and Training, and use the stronger Supervisors pipeline as the anchor for broader implementation discipline.

Data note: The June 22 Training entries for Chile were placeholder values, so the report uses validated June 8 inputs for SouthCone Training analysis.

US-Canada Rank #6

US-Canada shows isolated execution wins, but the overall portfolio remains constrained by onboarding, data, client approval and ROI realization.

US-Canada ranks #6 overall. The region performs well in RTM Bot and has visible Datanativ progress, while Training and Hallo show selective traction. However, comments across both reporting cuts show recurring friction in user provisioning, data warehousing, client approval, and economic validation. The risk in this region is no longer just technical deployment; it is whether implementations convert into the expected financial return.

Leadership implication: Tighten portfolio prioritization around accounts with the clearest monetization path, while fast-tracking onboarding/provisioning and the remaining approvals for Supervisors, Training and QA.
